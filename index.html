Classification of Immunofluorescent staining patterns of glomerulus in kidney biopsy via convolutional neural network and vision transformer

Abstract

Immunofluorescence (IF) staining is a cornerstone of glomerular disease diagnosis, providing the immunological signature needed to distinguish immune-complex, anti-GBM, and pauci-immune injury patterns in kidney biopsy specimens. However, its interpretation remains subjective, produces substantial variability between pathologists, and adds considerable workload in a field already limited by a shortage of trained renal pathologists. Existing deep learning approaches to glomerular IF have relied exclusively on convolutional neural networks and have addressed no more than three label dimensions, leaving a gap for models capable of comprehensive, multi-dimensional classification. This study develops and compares two deep learning architectures, a convolutional neural network (CNN) and a Vision Transformer (ViT), to predict the staining patterns and intensity of glomerular IF across six clinically meaningful output dimensions: Intensity, Pattern, Location, Granular subtype, Specific pattern, and Involvement pattern. A dataset of 1,297 cases comprising 12,882 IF images, collected at Srinagarind Hospital, Khon Kaen University, is independently annotated by three experienced renal pathologists, with inter-rater reliability assessed through Cohen's Kappa prior to consensus labeling. Both architectures are configured with six output heads to predict all dimensions simultaneously, trained using case-level splitting and five-fold cross-validation stratified on Intensity, and evaluated with classification and regression metrics benchmarked against individual pathologists. It is hypothesized that the Vision Transformer will outperform the convolutional neural network through its global self-attention mechanism. Ultimately, this framework aims to reduce inter-observer variability, standardize IF interpretation, and lower the diagnostic workload in renal pathology.

Keywords: Immunofluorescence (IF), Glomerulus, Kidney Biopsy, Deep Learning, Convolutional Neural Network, Vision Transformer
